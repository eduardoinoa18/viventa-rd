# VIVENTA Platform Market Leadership Roadmap 2026

Last updated: 2026-04-01
Owner: Product + Engineering + Operations
Scope: CRM, portal, MLS, constructora growth, and DR real-estate ecosystem leadership

## North Star

Build the dominant real-estate operating system for the Dominican Republic:

- Best CRM for brokers and agents in the DR
- Best real-estate search portal for buyers and renters
- Best MLS and inventory workspace for professionals
- Best promotional and sell-through platform for constructoras
- Best network for every serious participant in the DR property market

This means VIVENTA must win on four fronts at the same time:

1. More trust than classifieds
2. Better workflow than generic CRMs
3. Better inventory distribution than fragmented broker channels
4. Better local DR execution than imported global products

## Current Strengths To Protect

These are already meaningful advantages and should be treated as strategic assets:

- Multi-role architecture already exists across buyer, agent, broker, constructora, admin, and master admin surfaces
- Custom Firestore search avoids third-party dependency cost and gives product control
- Public professional profiles, verification concepts, reviews, and role-based dashboards already exist
- DR-specific transaction and reservation concepts are already present in important workflow areas
- Listing creation and project inventory foundations are stronger than a simple portal and closer to operating software
- Lead routing, inbox, notifications, and broker/agent dashboards already provide an execution base

## Market Leadership Gaps

The platform is strong enough to operate, but not yet strong enough to dominate. The main gaps are:

### 1. CRM gap

Current state: lead and task workflows exist, but the platform still behaves more like a lead tracker than a full DR brokerage operating system.

Must add:

- A true deal or opportunity entity from inquiry to reservation to contract to close
- SLA automation, smart follow-up logic, and broker-assignment intelligence
- Buyer timeline with notes, tasks, documents, financing, and showing history in one place

### 2. Portal gap

Current state: search is good, but not yet category-defining.

Must add:

- Richer search UX with sorting, histogram and slider controls, URL-persisted filters, and better saved-search automation
- Listing performance insights for professionals
- Recommendation and matching loops that make the marketplace feel alive

### 3. MLS gap

Current state: listing workspace exists, but not yet as a daily command center for power users.

Must add:

- Bulk actions, stronger filters, quality management, inventory operations, and listing analytics
- Team collaboration around listings, co-broke opportunities, and internal notes
- Better listing lifecycle governance for active, draft, pending, reserved, sold, and archived states

### 4. Constructora gap

Current state: project and reservation structures exist, but constructoras still need a growth engine, not only a management panel.

Must add:

- Project campaign tools, dynamic promotional offers, audience targeting, and lead routing controls
- Buyer financing and reservation intelligence
- Unit absorption, payment-plan, and sales-velocity analytics

### 5. Ecosystem gap

Current state: professionals can exist in the system, but network effects are still underdeveloped.

Must add:

- Searchable professional directory and better public discovery
- Referral, collaboration, and co-selling mechanics
- Shared industry trust signals, credentials, and performance proof

## Product Principles For The DR

Every major feature should be tested against these local-market rules:

1. WhatsApp-first operations are mandatory.
2. Reservation before contract must be a first-class workflow.
3. Listings, brokers, agents, and constructoras must feel verified and accountable.
4. City, sector, and project granularity matter more than generic global abstractions.
5. Teams need office-scoped collaboration and permission safety.
6. Buyers need confidence, clarity, and speed, not just inventory volume.

- Showing-to-reservation rate
- Reservation-to-contract rate

- Search-to-detail CTR

- Verified professionals by city
- Referral volume

- Partner-service attachment rate

## What Not To Do

Avoid these traps even while aiming high:

- Do not add generic enterprise complexity with no DR workflow payoff
- Do not build isolated tools that do not strengthen the capture-to-close loop
- Do not over-index on visual polish while key workflow metrics remain weak
- Do not fragment data models for leads, buyers, tasks, deals, and reservations
- Do not ship role experiences that diverge in terminology or state model
